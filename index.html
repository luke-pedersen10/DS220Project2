Question 1:

The first question I want to answer, is how much of a correlation is there between potential and the value of a player?

You may be wondering, why do I care? Player transfers work differently in soccer than they do in the NFL or NBA. In the NFL, if I want to acquire a player, I will send the other team one of my players and/or a draft pick. In soccer, if I want to aquire a player from a different club, I would send them a transfer fee to aquire the players contract.

import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
In [ ]:
np.random.seed(19680801)

fig, ax = plt.subplots()


ax.scatter(data['potential'], data['value_euro'], alpha=0.3, edgecolors='none')

ax.grid(True)
plt.xlabel('Potential')
plt.ylabel('Value (Euros)')
plt.title('Potential vs Value in Euros')

plt.show()

We see above, that there is only a correlation between potential and value, when we look at players with a 70 potential or above. It then begins to look like a growth function.

Below I sampled every 50 entries of my csv file, and then created a scatter plot showing the overall of the players on the x-axis, and the height on the y-axis, with the dots on the plot color coded to different positions.

In [ ]:
sample1 = data.iloc[::50, :]
positions = sample1['positions'].unique()
plt.figure(figsize=(10, 5))

for position in positions:
    subset = sample1[sample1['positions'] == position]
    plt.scatter(subset['overall_rating'], subset['height_cm'], label=position)

plt.xlabel('Overall')
plt.ylabel('Height (cm)')
plt.title('Height vs Overall by Position')
plt.legend()

plt.show()

What does this plot tell us? It tells us that, aside from goalkeepers, height doesn't have much of a correlation to how good a player is.
